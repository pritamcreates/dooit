    21 days — starting today
    The author's rule. You're allowed to miss one day in 21 — but never two in a row. Two missed days is a new habit forming: the habit of quitting. One miss is human. Two is a pattern. Don't let it become a pattern.
    Identity shift, not motivation. The goal isn't to do the thing. It's to become the person who does the thing. After 21 days, you don't need to decide whether to do it — it's just who you are. Start with that identity today.

    How it works

    Three steps
    01

    Define it precisely

    Vague habits fail. "Exercise more" never sticks. "15 minutes of movement before 8am" does. Write what done actually means — and commit to that definition for 21 days, not a watered-down version of it.

    →
    02

    Check in every day

    Mark each day done — or honest. You can note what happened, how you felt, what made it hard. The chain builds identity. Every ✓ is evidence you're becoming the person you said you'd be.

    →
    03

    Review and lock it in

    On day 21, review your chain. See your mood trend, your completion rate, your best notes. Then decide: extend it, level it up, or set a new challenge. You've earned the right to go bigger.

    The philosophy

    Built on the same system that creates world-class performers.

    ✓

    One habit at a time, always. Every top performer I've studied chose depth over breadth. They picked one thing and went absurdly deep on it. That's what 21 days is: deliberate, focused, singular.

    ✓

    The chain is the point. You're not doing this for the outcome. You're doing this for the chain. A 15-day unbroken chain has more psychological power than any motivational speech. Don't break it.

    ✓

    Honest data, not performed progress. Log your skips. Note your bad days. The people who hide their failures are the ones who repeat them. Honest tracking is the fastest path to real improvement.

    ✓

    Your "why" is your fuel — keep it visible. When motivation dies (and it will, around day 8), it's your reasons that carry you. That's why you wrote them down. That's why this tool shows them to you every single day.
